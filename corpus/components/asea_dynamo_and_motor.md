# Component: ASEA Dynamo and Induction Motor

## ASEA DC Dynamo (Generator)
- **Installation:** Added May 27, 1918.
- **Specifications:** 115 V – 160 V output, approx. 3 kW power (absorbing ~5 kW mechanical power from the engine). Runs at 1550 RPM.
- **Construction:** Extremely heavy (over 200 kg). Features a Gramme ring armature (patented 1871), ensuring a very steady DC voltage output with minimal fluctuation over time.
- **Role:** The heart of the DC generation system. Originally belt-driven by the Crossley engine, it converts mechanical energy into DC electrical energy to power the estate's 110V DC appliances and charge the battery bank.

## ASEA 3-Phase Induction Motor
- **Installation:** Installed December 6, 1929.
- **Specifications:** 3 horsepower (hp), 380 V / 120 V (Star connection, without neutral), 50 Hz, 1410 RPM.
- **Role:** Introduced when the public AC grid reached Chamusca. This motor replaced the Crossley engine as the mechanical prime mover for the dynamo. 
- **Operation:** The AC grid powers the induction motor. The motor spins, driving the ASEA DC dynamo via belt. The dynamo generates DC power to charge the batteries and supply the estate's legacy 110V DC circuits. This setup (AC motor driving a DC generator) functions as an early rotary converter.
