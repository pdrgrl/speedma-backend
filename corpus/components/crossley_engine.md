# Component: Crossley Internal Combustion Engine

## Specifications
- **Type:** Single-cylinder internal combustion engine (spark ignition via magneto).
- **Estimated Power:** 5 to 10 horsepower (hp).
- **Flywheels:** Two massive inertia flywheels, weighing over 500 kg each, necessary to maintain rotational momentum between combustion strokes.
- **Fuel:** Highly versatile; typical for the era, it could burn oils, paraffin, and other liquid fuels.

## Role in the System
Originally installed before 1918, the Crossley engine's primary purpose was mechanical: pumping water from the estate's well (mina) to an elevated reservoir for agricultural irrigation. 

In the **DC Electrification Phase (c. 1918)**, the engine gained a dual purpose. It was coupled via a belt drive to the newly installed ASEA DC dynamo. When the engine was running (e.g., for pumping water), it would simultaneously spin the dynamo, generating electricity for the estate.

## Simulation Context
- **Scenario A:** The engine is OFF. The house draws power entirely from the battery bank.
- **Scenario B:** The engine is ON, driving the dynamo via belt to charge the battery bank and power the house.
- **Scenario C:** The engine is disconnected from the electrical generation loop. It returns to purely mechanical duties, while the new AC induction motor takes over driving the dynamo.
