# Component: Tudor Lead-Acid Battery Bank

## Specifications
- **Manufacturer:** Sociedade Portuguesa do Acumulador Tudor, SARL (Order No. 979).
- **Type:** Open-cell lead-acid battery, Type L1.
- **Configuration:** 60 individual glass cells connected in series.
- **Capacity:** 36 Ah per cell (at a continuous discharge of 3.6 A). Total battery capacity approx 4.56 kVA.
- **Weight:** Each cell weighs roughly 10 kg dry. Total bank weight exceeds 700 kg.

## Voltage Characteristics
- Fully charged cell: 2.11 V
- Charging cell: 2.01 V
- Discharged cell: 1.93 V
- Total bank voltage (60 cells): 126.6 V (charged) / 115.8 V (discharged).

## Maintenance and Operation
Because these are open cells, maintenance requires visually inspecting the electrolyte and manually adding distilled water (if density is high) or sulfuric acid (if density is low).
- **Charging:** Recommend 9 A charge current. Maintain until gases visibly bubble from the electrodes uniformly across all cells.
- **Electrolyte Density:** Fully discharged density is 1.17; fully charged density is 1.20.

## Role in the System
Installed between 1921 and 1923, the battery bank provided energy storage, allowing the estate to have electricity 24 hours a day without running the Crossley engine continuously.
